Sims

  • Like the home page mentions, it's difficult to think without having some sort of back and forth. You can approximate the back and forth by using Sims.
  • A Sim is a simulation of someone you're pretending to be. In the example below, I pretend to be my future self and say, "You're such an idiot" to System I.
Usage: when a statement is clicked on/active, press cmd + ctrl + j to toggle the Sim that it belongs to. Alternatively, click the Sim to toggle it (the ovals that the arrows point to in the image above).
  • To anyone who's read Harry Potter and the Methods of Rationality, think of Harry's inner Gryffindor, Slytherin, Hufflepuff and Ravenclaw as Sims. Other examples include his simulations of people he respects like Hermoine and Quirrell.
  • I think it's useful to use Sims that oppose one another so you don't get too one-sided.
  • One subtle benefit I see to using Sims is that it permits you to pursue lines of thought that you otherwise wouldn't pursue. In the example below, without Sims, I normally wouldn't pursue the "Ugh, I'm better than that" line of thought because I label it as wrong and stupid. But if it's System I that's making the point and not "me", I feel better about pursuing it.
